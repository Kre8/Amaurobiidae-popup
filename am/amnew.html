Eyes
Eyes
    Always the polite way to start introductions; looking into our new friend's eyes will often provide clues as to which family they may belong.

    Two rows of small, evenly-sized, eyes make Amaurobiidae. Their eyes are right up front on handsomely open faces. Their heads (*cephalothorax) are both rounded and squared in the impossible fashion attained by wombats.

3 Claws
3 Claws
The Cribellum, when present, is located underneath the spinnerets, it has a similar appearance to the rough pad of a cats' paw. The Cribellum and Calamistrum, like violin and bow, are used together to pluck the fine silk into a 'wooly' texture. Not all spiders have a cribellum, not even all Amaurobiids have one.

At one stage all cribellate spiders were placed into a single family, this was further refined to full and divided cribellum. Eventually it was not regarded as the sole diagnostic and members were placed into other families.

(Lehtinen_1967)



V. T. Davies' papers seems to suggest all tasmanian genera are ecribellate (without cribellum).

    After a lengthy search little was revealed on the jaw particulars of Tassy Amaurobiids. Davies papers supply only anecdotal descriptions of chelicera. Vernon Victor Hickman's 1981 paper, however provides a line drawing of Oztira affinus. From this an approximate recreation could be made in 3d model form.

    A few paragraphs from Hichman's work:'Chelicerae slightly geniculate, with lateral condyles. Clothed in front with a few long setae.

    Furrow is oblique, promargin with six teeth, the distal four shorter than the others, retromargin with two teeth. Promarginal scapula of ten barbed setae (not included in picture or model). Fang well curved.'
